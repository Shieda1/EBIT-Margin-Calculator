What is the EBIT Margin?
ebit margin is a measurement of the firm's profitability and it is the EBIT over the revenue. It allows inbestors to understand the true costs of running a firm.
How to calculate EBIT Margin?
To calculate EBIT Margin you should use the following formula:
EBIT Margin = EBIT / revenue
where:
EBIT = revenue - cost of goods sold (COGS) - operating expenses
example: company x has a revenue of $5,300,000, $2,700,000 worth of cost of goods sold (COGS) and $1,600,000 in operating expenses, calculate the EBIT margin?
first we should calculate the EBIT.
EBIT = revenue - cost of goods sold (COGS) - operating expenses
EBIT = $5,300,000 - $2,700,000 - $1,600,000
EBIT = $1,000,000
now let's calculate the EBIT margin
EBIT Margin = EBIT / revenue
EBIT Margin = $1,000,000 / $5,300,000
EBIT Margin = 18.86%
Why use EBIT Margin?
EBIT margin is usually used by investors to understand the true costs of running a firm because some of the firm's equipment will eventually need to be replace when they are broken down or decayed.
the lower the EBIT margins the lower the profitability from a firm
